## ✅ 5 DO'S – Responsible Social Media Use

| # | ✅ DO | Why It Matters |
|---|-------|---------------|
| 1 | **Do keep your profiles professional and consistent** | Employers, professors, and internship coordinators search your name online. A professional profile creates a positive first impression. |
| 2 | **Do think before you post — ask "Would I show this to my professor or future employer?"** | Once posted, content is permanent. Even deleted posts can be screenshotted and shared. Your digital footprint follows you for life. |
| 3 | **Do use privacy settings to control who sees your personal posts** | Regularly review your privacy settings on Instagram, Facebook, and Snapchat to ensure only trusted people see personal content. |
| 4 | **Do use LinkedIn actively to build your professional network** | Connect with professors, seniors, and industry professionals. Share achievements, certifications, and learning milestones. |
| 5 | **Do report and block accounts that harass, bully, or threaten you** | Every major platform has a report button. Use it. Cyberbullying is a crime in India under the IT Act, 2000. |

---

## ❌ 5 DON'TS – Things to Avoid on Social Media

| # | ❌ DON'T | Why It Is Harmful |
|---|---------|------------------|
| 1 | **Don't share your personal details publicly — phone number, address, college ID, or Aadhaar** | Sharing personal details publicly exposes you to identity theft, stalking, and fraud. Cybercriminals actively harvest this information. |
| 2 | **Don't post offensive, discriminatory, or hateful content of any kind** | Such posts can result in disciplinary action by your college, damage your reputation permanently, and may even attract legal consequences. |
| 3 | **Don't accept friend or follow requests from unknown people** | Unknown contacts can use your personal photos, location, and information for malicious purposes including impersonation and phishing. |
| 4 | **Don't share your OTP, UPI PIN, passwords, or banking details on any platform — including WhatsApp and DMs** | No bank, company, or government agency will EVER ask for your OTP or PIN online. This is always a scam. |
| 5 | **Don't post your real-time location, travel plans, or home address publicly** | Sharing live location or travel plans puts your physical safety at risk and can make you a target for theft or stalking. |

---

## 💡 Key Takeaway

> *"Your social media presence is your digital reputation. Build it with the same care you would your professional resume — because in today's world, employers look at both."*
