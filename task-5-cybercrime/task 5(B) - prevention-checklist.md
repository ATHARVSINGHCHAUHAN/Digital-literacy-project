## 🔐 8 Must-Follow Online Safety Tips for College Students in India

---

### ✅ Tip 1 – Use Strong, Unique Passwords for Every Account
Create passwords that are at least 12 characters long and include a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters (e.g., @, #, $). Never use the same password for two different accounts. Use a password manager like Bitwarden or Google Password Manager to store them safely.

**Example of a WEAK password:** `atharv123`
**Example of a STRONG password:** `At@rv#2025!VIT`

---

### ✅ Tip 2 – Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on All Important Accounts
Turn on 2FA for your Gmail, GitHub, LinkedIn, and any banking apps. Even if someone steals your password, they cannot log in without the second verification step (usually an OTP sent to your phone). This single step prevents most account takeovers.

---

### ✅ Tip 3 – Never Click on Unknown or Suspicious Links
Before clicking any link in an email, SMS, or WhatsApp message, hover over it to check the actual URL. Legitimate websites use HTTPS. Phishing links often mimic real websites with slight spelling differences (e.g., `paytm-support.com` instead of `paytm.com`). When in doubt — do not click.

---

### 💰 Tip 4 – NEVER Share Your UPI PIN, OTP, or Bank Password with Anyone
*(UPI-Specific Safety Tip)*

Your UPI PIN is like your ATM PIN — it authorises money leaving your account. No bank, customer care agent, government official, or Google Pay/PhonePe representative will EVER ask for your UPI PIN or OTP. If anyone asks — hang up immediately and call your bank's official helpline.

> **Remember: Entering your UPI PIN means YOU are SENDING money. You never need your PIN to RECEIVE money.**

---

### 💰 Tip 5 – Always Verify Before Paying or Accepting UPI Requests
*(UPI-Specific Safety Tip)*

Before making or accepting any UPI transaction, verify the recipient's name shown in the app. When selling something online, always confirm you have RECEIVED the money in your bank account or UPI app before handing over the item — do not trust screenshots of payments. A payment request notification is NOT the same as receiving money.

---

### ✅ Tip 6 – Avoid Using Public or Unknown WiFi Networks for Banking and Sensitive Activities
Free WiFi at cafes, bus stands, or public places can be insecure or even set up by hackers to intercept your data. Never log in to your bank account, UPI app, or college portal on public WiFi. Use your mobile data for any sensitive transactions or login activities.

---

### ✅ Tip 7 – Keep Your Phone, Apps, and Operating System Updated
Software updates fix security vulnerabilities that hackers actively exploit. Enable automatic updates for your phone's operating system and all installed apps — especially banking apps and browsers. An unpatched app is an open door for malware.

---

### ✅ Tip 8 – Regularly Review App Permissions and Revoke Unnecessary Access
Many apps ask for permissions they don't need — a flashlight app asking for access to your contacts or location is a red flag. Go to your phone's Settings → Apps → Permissions and review which apps have access to your camera, microphone, location, SMS, and contacts. Revoke permissions that seem unnecessary.

## 📞 If You Are a Victim of Cybercrime — Report Immediately

| Resource | Details |
|----------|---------|
| 🌐 **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File a complaint online |
| 📞 **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** — Available 24x7, Government of India |
| 🏦 **Bank Helpline** | Call your bank's official customer care immediately to freeze your account |
| 👮 **Local Police** | File an FIR at your nearest police station for financial fraud |

> ⚡ **Act fast!** Report UPI fraud within the first hour — banks can sometimes reverse transactions if reported quickly enough.
